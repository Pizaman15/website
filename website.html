History of VR

Brief History

Many may believe that the idea and development of VR is quite recent, for Virtual Reality gaming, movie or television streaming have made Virtual Reality very popular. But VR's history and construction begins in the 1950's. VR's displays have become much more mainstream now with companies like Oculus, HTC, and others using Virtual Reality for gaming, adding a whole new layer to the way people play video games at their homes. The man believed to have layed the ground work for VR was filmaker Morton Heilig. His Sensorama was a large booth like machine that would fit one to four people, combining technologies for smell, sound, and vibrations, there was even atmospheric effects like wind that would blow in someones hair. The first actual head mounted display was invinted by Ivan Sutherland. The HMD was connected to a sterioscopic diplay connested to a computer program that dipicted simple geometric shapes.

Recent History

The VR technologies from the 50's and 60's are not compareable to the VR technologies today. Today people now have wireless VR headsets and controllers, which allow for endless freedom. Tredmills specialy designed to hold the user and allow for imersive control over running and walking speed, and crouching. Graphics for VR is more than aceptable now, with 1080p resolution and Oled panels in some displays, giving realistic and imersive colors and visuals. VR although well known for its gaming aspect, VR has educational and practical uses in todays age. Schools can now afford to buy multiple machines and students can use them for presentations such as artwork or other graphical designs made within VR. Video game development studieos can use VR to create character models and other models for their games useing 3D graphic designing. While VR's technologie has evolved exponentialy, we can expect to see much more in the coming years.

Effects of VR

Although VR can be one of the most immursive forms of entertainment, it has a few minor setbacks.

VR Sickness

VR sickness summarizes most of the negative effects after gaming with VR for a long amount of time. It is called VR sickness because it relates to an actual sickness. Most times, you take off the headset and you become very disoriented and dizzy. This is one of the most common short term effects. Another effect is nausia if you suffer from motion sickness.

Long Term Effects

In todays world it is unclear what long term effects may be. Most believe that there will be no effects in the long run so game on!
